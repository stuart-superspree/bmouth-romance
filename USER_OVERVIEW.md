# The Romance Writing Companion App — User Overview

A simple guide to what the app does, why each part is there, and how it is meant to be used.

## What it is

A free, phone friendly web app for everyone attending the **Romance Writing Festival** (Saturday 3 October 2026, Marsham Court Hotel, Bournemouth). It opens in any web browser, with nothing to install and no account to create.

Unlike a typical event app that is only useful on the day, this one is built to help **before, during and after** the festival, so it stays valuable as a year round writing companion.

Everything a person enters (their notes, plans, saved people and so on) is stored privately **on their own phone**. They can close the app and come back later and it will be exactly as they left it. Nothing is sent anywhere.

Two principles guided the build:

- **No AI.** Romance writers are often advised against using AI, and the festival asks that submissions are a writer's own words. So the app uses only hand written prompts and simple matching, never generated text.
- **Built around the real festival.** The schedule, speakers, agents and deadlines all come from this year's actual programme, so the guidance is specific and useful.

## How the app is intended to be used, and who it helps

The app is designed to do real work at three moments. Each moment helps the **attendee** get more from the festival, and also quietly helps the **festival organisers** run a smoother, more connected, more memorable event.

### Before the festival — prepare and connect

**Intended use:** in the weeks leading up, an attendee sets up a short profile, browses the programme and chooses which sessions to attend, finds other writers they would like to meet, and prepares for the things that reward preparation, such as an agent meeting or the live first page critique. A countdown and a list of real deadlines keep the important dates in view.

**How it helps attendees:** they arrive ready rather than overwhelmed. They already know which sessions they want, who they hope to meet, and what they want to ask, so the day feels purposeful from the first minute.

**How it helps organisers:** preparation reduces day of confusion and last minute questions. Surfacing deadlines (such as agent submission and the Slush Pile entry) encourages more, and better prepared, entries. Building a sense of who else is coming creates anticipation and a warmer room on the day, and early profile sign ups give a head start on a genuine attendee community.

### During the festival — navigate, learn and meet

**Intended use:** on the day the app is a personal guide. It helps the attendee choose between the two parallel rooms at each time slot, find the right room, keep private notes and a star rating against each session, jot questions to ask, and meet the people on their list.

**How it helps attendees:** the hardest practical decision of the day is "which of the two rooms now?", and the app makes that easy and personal. Because the sessions are not recorded, the notes a writer takes here become the lasting record of everything they learned.

**How it helps organisers:** clear, personal guidance eases the flow between rooms and gently manages the popular, limited capacity Talks Room by reminding people to arrive early, which reduces crowding and disappointment. Attendees who can self serve answers about timings, rooms and what is on ask fewer questions of a busy team, and writers who have already identified who to meet make the networking moments livelier and more productive.

### After the festival — reflect, act and stay connected

**Intended use:** in the days and weeks afterwards, the attendee captures a short reflection, sets a simple 30, 60 and 90 day writing plan, follows up with the people they met, and exports a personal summary of their whole festival to keep.

**How it helps attendees:** the inspiration of a single day is turned into momentum and finished pages, and the connections made do not fade once everyone goes home.

**How it helps organisers:** the festival's value extends well beyond the day, which strengthens word of mouth and the case for returning next year. The app naturally points people towards the festival's year round community (such as the writers' Facebook group, the April Bournemouth Writing Festival and the Writing Sanctuary), helping grow the audience that funds the festival's free community work. Keeping the attendee cohort warm and connected also makes each future event easier to fill.

## The five areas of the app

There are five areas along the bottom of the screen. Here is what each one is for and why it earns its place.

### Home

A welcoming front page that changes depending on how close the festival is: a countdown and preparation checklist before, what is on and how to find your room during, and reflection and follow up prompts after. It also shows a **Festival Passport** of badges, a gentle and optional way to encourage people to explore the app and make the most of the day.

### Schedule

The day at a glance, built around the festival's real structure of two rooms running at once. Attendees see both rooms side by side and choose one, mark favourites, tick off sessions attended, give a star rating, and write private notes and questions against each. The app flags that the Talks Room is limited and to arrive early. Because none of the sessions are recorded, these notes are the only record a writer will keep.

### People

Two things in one place: who to learn from and who to meet. **Speakers** lists every speaker with their role, their session and a few ready made conversation starters. **Writers** lets an attendee create an opt in mini profile and browse and save other attendees by what they are looking for, such as a critique partner or beta readers. Meeting other writers is the thing attendees most want, and this lets those connections start before the day and continue after it.

### Write

A small toolkit for preparing and practising: a **Pitch Builder** for a hook, elevator pitch and query; an **Agent Matcher** that lines a writer's genres up against the six visiting agents' wish lists; **Slush Pile Prep** for polishing the first 500 words; and a **Writing Sprint** timer, **Flash Challenges** and **Trope Sparks** for practice and ideas. Everything here supports preparation and practice, and is never a substitute for the writer's own work.

### More

Everything else, kept tidy: the **festival guide and FAQs**, ticket and venue information, a **resource library**, a **festival reflection** and **30/60/90 day action plan**, a view of **progress and connections**, **profile and settings**, and an **export** that saves the writer's whole festival summary as a file to keep.

## Look and feel

The app uses the festival's own branding: the heart and open book logo, the sunshine yellow with blue and white, and a clean, readable, book inspired style. The aim is something warm and welcoming but still professional, in keeping with a literary festival.

## One thing not yet switched on

The writer directory currently shows a few sample profiles so people can see how it works. When the festival is ready, it can be connected to a live, opt in list so real attendees appear, with the festival able to manage it. A writer's saved list and the rest of the app keep working exactly the same either way.
